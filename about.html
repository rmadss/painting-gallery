Statement

I paint slowly. Most of my work comes from looking at ordinary things for longer than feels comfortable — a lamp left on after dark, the specific quality of winter light on a rooftop, the geometry of a doorway nobody else seems to notice.

I'm not trying to make beautiful objects. I'm trying to record something true about a moment — the temperature of the light, the weight of the silence. Whether I succeed is a separate question.

Approach

Most paintings start with a single observation I can't let go of. I work from life when I can and from photographs when I can't — though I try to use photographs the way a writer uses notes, not as something to copy.

I'm drawn to the moment just before or after something happens: the empty chair, the unlit room, the street an hour before anyone wakes up. There's a stillness in those moments that I find more interesting than drama.

I'm not attached to a single medium. I use whatever gets closest to what I'm after. Oil for its slowness, acrylic when I need to work faster, the occasional watercolor sketch to loosen up.

Materials

What's currently on the table:

  • Oils Williamsburg, Gamblin — titanium white, raw umber, burnt sienna, ivory black, ultramarine, yellow ochre
  • Acrylics Golden Heavy Body for larger work that needs faster drying
  • Surfaces Linen (preferred), cotton canvas, wood panel for smaller pieces
  • Brushes Mostly flats and filberts — Silver Brush, Rosemary & Co.
  • Medium Linseed oil, occasionally Gamsol for cleaning and lean layers
